Uncertainty quantification for molecular solubility

Which solubility predictions can we trust? Calibration, UQ, and uncertainty-guided data collection in a chemistry setting.

// image placeholder — calibration curves

Motivation

Molecular solubility prediction is a setting where a confident wrong number can mislead downstream decisions. The interesting question isn't just "how accurate is the model" but "which predictions carry real confidence, and is that confidence calibrated?"

What I'm looking at

  • Comparing UQ approaches (ensembles, MC dropout, etc.) on solubility benchmarks.
  • Measuring calibration and sharpness, not just RMSE.
  • Using uncertainty to guide which new molecules to label — active learning for chemistry.
